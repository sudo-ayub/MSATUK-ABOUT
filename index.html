ABOUT MSATUK

The Muslim Students Association of the Technical University of Kenya (MSATUK) is a student-led body that represents and serves the Muslim community within the Technical University of Kenya. It was established to provide a platform where Muslim students can uphold their faith, strengthen their identity, and thrive both academically and socially.

MSATUK stands as the central hub for Muslim students at the Technical University of Kenya, offering a strong sense of community and belonging. It is more than just an association — it is a family built on the principles of brotherhood, sisterhood, and mutual support. Within this community, students come together to strengthen their faith, share knowledge and ideas, and support one another in both academic and personal pursuits. By creating a platform for collaboration, mentorship, and growth, MSATUK empowers its members to reach their potential while fostering unity and solidarity. In doing so, the association ensures that Muslim students not only thrive individually but also contribute collectively to the development of the wider university community

Beyond the campus, MSATUK actively participates in outreach and charitable initiatives, reflecting the Islamic principle of serving humanity. By combining faith with action, the association strives to nurture responsible, visionary, and well-rounded individuals who can contribute positively to society.

OUR MISSION

The mission of the Muslim Students Association of the Technical University of Kenya (MSATUK) is to provide a supportive and inclusive platform for Muslim students to practice their faith, strengthen their identity, and excel in all aspects of life. Guided by Islamic values, we are committed to fostering unity, promoting brotherhood and sisterhood, and nurturing both academic and spiritual growth.

MSATUK strives to create opportunities for learning, leadership, and service, empowering students to contribute positively to the university and to society at large. Through worship, knowledge-sharing, and community engagement, our mission is to develop well-rounded individuals who embody faith, integrity, and excellence.

VISION

To be a beacon of unity and growth for Muslim students at the Technical University of Kenya, shaping well-rounded individuals who uphold Islamic values, excel in knowledge, and contribute to the betterment of the university and beyond.

OBJECTIVES

Key principles and aims:

Faith & Identity

To create an enabling environment where Muslim students can freely and confidently practice their faith, uphold Islamic values, and strengthen their spiritual identity.

Unity & Community

To foster a strong sense of brotherhood and sisterhood among Muslim students, promoting mutual support, inclusivity, and solidarity within the university.

Academic & Personal Excellence

To provide platforms for academic mentorship, leadership development, and personal growth that enable students to thrive both in their studies and in life beyond the university.

Knowledge & Learning

To facilitate continuous learning through study circles, seminars, workshops, and knowledge-sharing initiatives that nurture intellectual and spiritual growth.

Leadership & Service

To cultivate principled and visionary leaders among Muslim students who can serve the university, the nation, and the global community with integrity and excellence.

Welfare & Support

To address the welfare needs of Muslim students, offering guidance, counseling, and assistance that enhances their well-being and university experience.

Outreach & Community Engagement

To engage in charitable, social, and outreach programs that reflect the Islamic principle of serving humanity and contributing positively to society.

Collaboration & Representation

To represent the interests of Muslim students within the Technical University of Kenya and collaborate with other student bodies, institutions, and organizations for mutual benefit.

1. Chairman & Chairlady

  • Lead and represent MSATUK inside and outside the university.
  • Guide all activities, ensure they align with Islamic values.
  • Build relationships with other student bodies and the university.
  • Chair meetings and make final decisions when needed.

2. Secretary General

  • Coordinates daily operations of MSATUK.
  • Keeps membership records and communication platforms updated.
  • Works with Chairlady to organize events and meet students’ needs.
  • Acts as the link with the university and SATUK on official matters.

3. Secretary & Assistant Secretary

  • Handle meeting agendas, minutes, and documentation.
  • Call meetings and share information with members.
  • Ensure smooth communication between executives and general members.

4. Treasurer & Assistant Treasurer

  • Manage MSATUK finances (budgeting, collections, and expenses).
  • Keep records of all transactions.
  • Oversee fundraising activities and report on financial status.

5. Imam & Mwadhiin

  • Lead prayers and ensure proper timekeeping of salah.
  • Organize Islamic Awareness Week and da’wah activities.
  • Keep the mosque clean and orderly.
  • Provide guidance on Islamic matters.

6. Year Representatives (1st – 6th Year)

  • Represent students of their year group.
  • Collect membership and contribution fees.
  • Help organize activities and share information with their classmates.
  • Support executives in data collection and event participation.

COMMUNITY IMPACT

MSATUK is committed to embodying the principle of faith in action by positively impacting both the university and the wider community. Our initiatives extend beyond campus, reflecting our dedication to service, compassion, and social responsibility.

Orphanage Visits: Through regular visits to orphanages, MSATUK members share their time, care, and resources with vulnerable children, fostering hope, joy, and a sense of belonging. These initiatives nurture empathy among students while strengthening the bond between the association and the wider community.

Student Support Programs: Within the university, MSATUK provides financial assistance and fundraising support for students facing tuition challenges. By ensuring that no Muslim student is left behind due to financial hardship, the association promotes inclusivity, academic success, and solidarity.

Mosque Cleaning: In line with the Islamic value of honoring places of worship, MSATUK organizes mosque cleaning initiatives, encouraging members to actively contribute to the upkeep of sacred spaces while instilling humility, teamwork, and respect for the house of Allah.

Volunteering in External Events: MSATUK members actively volunteer in external events — such as community service projects, interfaith dialogues, and social development programs — demonstrating the association’s commitment to being a positive force within society and strengthening ties beyond the university.

Through these efforts, MSATUK not only uplifts individuals in need but also cultivates a culture of giving, unity, and responsibility among its members — preparing them to become leaders who make a lasting impact in society.
